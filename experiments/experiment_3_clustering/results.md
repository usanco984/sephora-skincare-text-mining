# Experiment 3: Clustering Analysis

## Goal

The goal of this experiment was to discover hidden themes in customer reviews using unsupervised learning techniques.

K-means clustering was applied to group reviews into topics based on textual similarity.

Dimensionality reduction was performed using **Singular Value Decomposition (SVD)**.

---

## Data Preparation

To make clustering computationally manageable, a subset of the dataset was used.

Dataset used:

| Sample Size |
|------|
| 1000 reviews |

---

## Clustering Results

The clustering analysis revealed several meaningful themes in customer reviews.

| Cluster | Topic | Example Words |
|------|------|------|
| 0 | Acne / Dry Skin | acne, dry, skin |
| 1 | Moisturizing Products | cream, gel, moisturizer |
| 2 | Face Masks | mask, pores, smooth |
| 3 | Cleansing | cleanser, makeup, clean |
| 4 | Promotional Reviews | received, influenster, free |

---

## Key Findings

Several clusters clearly correspond to common skincare concerns.

Frequent discussion topics included:

- acne treatment
- dry skin care
- moisturizing products
- cleansing routines

An additional cluster contained words such as:

- received
- complimentary
- influenster
- free

---

## Interpretation

This cluster suggests that some reviews may be associated with **promotional campaigns or influencer programs** rather than purely organic customer feedback.

Recognizing these patterns is important when interpreting review sentiment and customer opinions.
